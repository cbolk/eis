Tri-generation

Less fuel, more energy

Trigeneration or combined cooling, heat and power (CCHP), is the process by which some of the heat produced by a cogeneration plant is used to generate chilled water for air conditioning or refrigeration. By capturing the excess heat, that would be wasted in a conventional power plant, CCHP potentially reach an efficiency of up to 80%, for the best conventional plants. This means that less fuel needs to be consumed to produce the same amount of useful energy.

Charging Stations

Energy in motion

A recharging station is an element of a Smart Grid that supplies electric energy for the recharging of electric vehicles. In Milan, charging stations are provided by A2A S.p.A. utility company and accesibles through a subscription. Recharging a large battery pack presents a high load on the electrical grid, but this can be scheduled for periods of reduced load or reduced electricity costs. In order to schedule the recharging, either the charging station or the vehicle can communicate with the smart grid. Some plug-in vehicles also allow the vehicle operator to control recharging through a web interface or mobile applications.
